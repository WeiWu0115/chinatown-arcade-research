# Open Coding: Chinatown ARcade User Interview Analysis

## Methodology
Line-by-line and segment-by-segment coding of 22 interview transcripts from participants who experienced the Chinatown ARcade AR experience. Codes are grounded in participants' own language (in-vivo codes marked with quotes). Each code is tagged with participant pseudonym(s).

---

## 1. INTERACTION & GESTURE

### 1.1 Gesture Intuitiveness
- **Pinch gesture feels natural**: "once you tell me how to interact... it all falls in place kind of naturally" (P01); "gestures register really well" (P01); "I really liked the gesture controls... very simple and intuitive" (P04)
- **Screenshot gesture recognized**: "I like the connection... very similar to like on the Mac" (P10); "I feel like this motion is very intuitive" (P10)
- **Pinch-and-zoom as familiar**: "how you pinch and zoom, how you pinch a button" (P01)

### 1.2 Gesture Difficulties
- **Photo capture inconsistency**: "sometimes it didn't register as consistently as I hoped for" (P01); "I was trying to take some pictures... maybe a little bit buggy" (P02); "it is kind of difficult to use... I have to repeat it for several times" (P16)
- **Lack of capture feedback**: "I wasn't sure if it was working or doing anything... I would do the motion and then wait a few seconds" (P07); "maybe add some trying to feedback after you take the picture" (P07)
- **Third-hand interference**: "when your hand appears, it will be directly positioned on the third hand" — staff hands causing tracking conflicts (P19)
- **Two-handed gesture awkwardness**: "it's really awkward to be holding something and also trying to do two-handed gestures" (P03)
- **3D model renders behind photo**: "the 3D model that's supposed to form formed actually behind the picture" (P15); "the item pop up behind the photo" (P21)

### 1.3 Object Placement Issues
- **Couldn't place object on card**: "I grabbed and then I couldn't place it on the card" (P18)
- **Throwing away image feels complex**: "after that I need to throw away the image... this process is kind of complicated" (P16)

## 2. NAVIGATION & WAYFINDING

### 2.1 Light Beam Navigation
- **Light beam not visible/unreliable**: "I saw it like once but for a second" (P10); "the yellow lines also did not appear for me until I was actually looking right where they were supposed to be" (P18)
- **Direction finding not accurate**: "nowadays it's just a light in front of you... that's not very accurate" (P05/P06)
- **Light column "thrown off"**: "the light columns could be... it's all like thrown off a little bit" (P09)

### 2.2 Alternative Navigation Ideas
- **Warmer/cooler system**: "almost like a warmer, cooler type of situation... green you're in the right direction, red you're going the wrong direction" (P10)
- **Digital landmark for orientation**: "cool maybe having like a digital landmark so you could orient yourself on the map" (P10)
- **Floating character guide**: team discusses "a floating dumpling... follow this little guy" (P10)
- **Sound-based navigation**: "incorporate sound... the sound effect would capture" (P11); "adding voice hints" (P11)
- **Heat map instead of pointer**: "maybe a heap map instead" (P10)

### 2.3 Clue/Hint System
- **Clues too vague**: "a couple of the clues were a little bit tricky" (P07); "I didn't know how to find the next one" (P19)
- **Progressive hint system desired**: "start with a broad hint... get a better hint to get you to the next level" (P09); "after three minutes it gives you the next hint" (P09); "like phone a friend" (P10)
- **Time-based hint reveal**: "maybe it's a time-based thing... after certain amount of time it just gets you another hint" (P09/P10)
- **Objects too far/high**: "some of the objects might be too far... image quality wasn't high enough for a far away object" (P04); "it's hard to find... you have to raise your head" (P19)
- **Game order confusion**: "from mailbox to ATM... the game tells me it's already over... the order seems to be a little bit not exact" (P02); "the third, however, in our game order is fourth" (P05/P06)

## 3. ONBOARDING & INSTRUCTIONS

### 3.1 Insufficient Initial Instructions
- **Confusion at start**: "I was a little confused. I didn't know what the process was" (P19); "it took me five, ten minutes to finally get it" (P22)
- **Need more app instructions**: "maybe a little more instructions from the app itself, instead of relying on a human volunteer" (P01)
- **Self-explanatory system needed**: "we can make it like self-explanatory so user can explore and enjoy on their own" (P20)
- **"No instruction, then tell me what to do"** (P19)

### 3.2 Onboarding Ideas
- **Video tutorial before starting**: "people in line can see the video... the user can call back again" (P19)
- **Printed instructions**: "if you have something printed, or show it on some pictures, it will be more convenient" (P19)
- **5-minute training session**: "before they actually get the goggles, there's like a little five minute training and then the tour starts" (P10)
- **Old video game analogy**: "even from the 80s... push the button three times... something very simple" (P10)
- **First experience as training**: "that first walkthrough was good enough where I feel very comfortable doing it again" (P21)

## 4. HARDWARE & COMFORT

### 4.1 Weight & Fit
- **Too heavy**: "it is too heavy for me to wear it... makes my ears hurt" (P16)
- **Tight on head**: "the glass is a little tight on the head, maybe it could be a little wider" (P01)
- **Acceptable for some**: "it's quite comfortable... a little bit bulky, but it's not too heavy for me" (P07); "they're fine... kind of cool looking" (P10)
- **Novel form factor appreciated**: "just trying on a compact, small form factor glass, it's a novel experience" (P01)

### 4.2 Field of View (FOV)
- **FOV too small**: "the FOV of the frames is pretty small, you have to look at the things actually" (P08); "I have to look slightly to the side to see all the VR stuff" (P18); "you cannot see something that is not in the center" (P16)

### 4.3 Technical Issues
- **Battery life**: "the hardware battery life is not the greatest" (P03)
- **Lens restart needed**: "I had to restart the lens a couple of times" (P03)
- **Glasses shut down early**: "glasses did shut down a little early... right before we got to our last object" (P17)
- **Geolocation finicky**: "geolocation is always finicky" (P03)
- **Wi-Fi dependency**: portable Wi-Fi mentioned as potential issue (P05, P15)
- **Image/vision instability**: "the image, the vision is not that stable" (P11)

### 4.4 Eye Calibration
- **IPD adjustment needed**: "ask people to have... the data of the distance between your eyes... improve the experience for everybody" (P11)

## 5. NARRATIVE & STORYTELLING

### 5.1 Desire for Deeper Narrative
- **Story potential recognized**: "I can see potential as it being a more narrative driven experience" (P03); "I would love to see more storyline" (P02)
- **Current narrative too basic**: "right now it was kind of basic... you find a clue, you go. There's really not much explanation" (P03)
- **Role cards underutilized**: "I know we have these roll cards... there's supposed to be a story element" (P03)
- **Continuity between elements lacking**: "the continuity between each element... how do I know the next one is in the corner" (P19)

### 5.2 Historical/Educational Value
- **Want to feel the past**: "I wanna feel what it was like to be on this street 100 years ago" (P13); "like a tenement museum" (P13)
- **Historical overlay desired**: "overlay the historical view of the same site... rendered as it was 100 years ago" (P20)
- **Archival photos expected**: "I thought through the glass you can see the archival photo of Chinatown" (P22)
- **Learning through exploration**: "I love the fact that we could learn a little bit about the area" (P10); "it really invites the community and everyone to just explore more" (P02)
- **Making invisible history visible**: "Chinatown has a lot of depth... this kind of immersive experience really can help you get deeper into the community" (P01)
- **Translation feature valued**: "I really liked how you could just take a picture of anything and then it would tell you about it" (P10)

### 5.3 Multiplayer/Social Narrative
- **Multiplayer desire**: "it would be really cool if we could interact in some way with the gangster and the police officer... stories intertwine" (P07)
- **Different roles, different perspectives**: mentioned by P03, P07, P10
- **Difficulty levels**: "you could have different variations of difficulty... level one, two, three" (P10)

## 6. EMBODIMENT & PRESENCE

### 6.1 Sense of Presence
- **Felt present in the world**: "I felt very present wearing these lenses, walking through the streets" (P04); "I didn't feel like I was going to run into them" (P04)
- **"Embodied visualization"**: "it's like embodied visualization of that process... we capture something with our eyes and retrieve something in our memory" (P20)
- **Bending to scan felt immersive**: "being able to actually go bend down and go like this, it felt like a very cool move to make" (P04)

### 6.2 Social Attention & Performance
- **Attracting curious pedestrians**: "the pedestrians were curious and they even came to us to ask what it is" (P20)
- **Feeling cool/special**: "people want to be different... people are like, are they famous or something?" (P10)
- **"We're on the leading edge of something"** (P10)
- **Social sharing opportunity**: "it's not only a personal experience... it's in a social setting, you catch attention" (P20)

### 6.3 Connection to Physical World
- **AR enhances real environment**: "leverage technology in a way that helps people build deeper relationships to the physical world" (P03)
- **Scavenger hunt mechanic**: "an innovative scavenger hunt... people love scavengers" (P10)
- **"Like Mario in real world"** (P12) — collecting coins/finding hidden gems
- **Photography as embodied action**: capturing clues = participation in narrative

## 7. SAFETY & ENVIRONMENT

### 7.1 Street Safety Concerns
- **Safety on busier streets**: "imagine on a busier intersection... you would want some sort of prompts or safety guidelines" (P01)
- **Pedestrian-only street was fine**: "this was a pedestrian-only street... you can walk around pretty much simply" (P01)

### 7.2 Staff & Social Environment
- **Staff presence helpful but needed**: "you still need someone to accompany me and explain" (P20); "if everyone has a staff with him, it should be much better" (P19)
- **Staff wearing uniform suggested**: "if there are staff wearing different clothes, it will give people a hint of what they are doing" (P19)
- **Pedestrian interference**: "the user will be disturbed by others during the use process" (P19)
- **Staff was gracious**: "everyone is really gracious... really kind on showing stuff and patient" (P18)

## 8. MONETIZATION & PRICING

### 8.1 Willingness to Pay (WTP)
- **$10-15**: P01 (10-15 min experience)
- **$10-20**: P09 (blind box); P16 ($10-20 basic, $30 with more)
- **$15-25**: P04 (15-30 min, with others)
- **$20**: P05/P06; P10 (for 30 min); P21
- **$20-30**: P09 (experience); P20 (for 10 min)
- **$25-30**: P02 (with more content)
- **$30**: P22 (standalone); $100+ combined with tour
- **$40-50**: P07 (multiplayer, longer); P10 (if solid UX)
- **$150-200**: P15 (combined with full tour, for tourist families)
- **$200-500**: P15 (educational/school programs)

### 8.2 Value Drivers
- **Duration matters**: longer = more willing to pay
- **Content depth**: more storyline, more objects = more value
- **Multiplayer**: willing to pay more for social experience
- **Uniqueness**: "first one in NYC" — no competition reference (P10)
- **Comparison to escape rooms**: "$50 for escape room" (P10)
- **Comparison to Viator tours**: "$150 for city tours" (P15)

### 8.3 Monetization Strategy Ideas
- **Partnership with city tourism**: "partnered with New York City's organization of tourism" (P15)
- **B2B over B2C initially**: "monetizations are going to come with co-labs with existing companies and not straight to user" (P15)
- **Restaurant/business partnerships**: "we could feature you in here... incentivizes people to actually go into the store" (P04)
- **Coupons/discounts post-experience**: "come back and use your card" (P10)
- **Education market**: "kids that want to use and create the experience themselves for a classroom project" (P15)
- **Lower price = repeat visits**: "if you charge 20, I might be more inclined to come back and do the other track" (P10)

## 9. SOUVENIRS & MERCHANDISE

### 9.1 Physical Souvenirs Desired
- **Blind boxes popular**: "I love blind boxes... not knowing what you're gonna get" (P09); but some dislike: "I'm not personally" into blind boxes (P13)
- **Keychains of collected items**: "one of the items I collected if it was like a keychain" (P09)
- **Functional over decorative**: "I'm more of a fan of things that are useful" (P13); "I don't need something that may be just trash later" (P13)
- **Food-based rewards**: "finishing at a dumpling house... ties cultural learning to food" (P13)
- **Plushies**: dumpling plush discussed (P10, shown in PDF)
- **VIP card for local vendors**: "the souvenir could be a VIP card for all the vendors on the street... 10% off" (P10)

### 9.2 Digital Souvenirs
- **AR postcard**: "this piece of paper... if you could with your phone... Web AR" (P10)
- **Completion badge**: "you completed this task and you get a badge" (P19)
- **3D model collection**: the postcard mechanic already serves as digital souvenir

### 9.3 Postcard as Souvenir Platform
- **3D map potential**: "if you look at the map like this, it's 3D... you could layer them up" (P10) — transform the physical card into a 3D AR experience
- **Tabletop AR game extension**: "AR could be tabletop games... this could be your tabletop" (P10)

## 10. CULTURAL SIGNIFICANCE & COMMUNITY

### 10.1 Cultural Heritage Value
- **Promoting Chinese culture**: "really good way to bring technology in and bring the culture out to the world" (P14); "really appreciate that people see this and see us and help to promote Chinese culture" (P14)
- **Old town rebirth**: "Chinatown is historical... with new technology, it gives the old town a rebirth chance, a new potential" (P22)
- **Personal connection**: "where my mom grew up" (P09)
- **"Making invisible history visible"**: core theme from design document

### 10.2 Tourism & Community Impact
- **Tourist attraction potential**: "Chinatown now thrives on tourism and whatever new experience will be an add-on" (P22)
- **Supporting local businesses**: partnership with local restaurants, bakeries (P04, P10, P13)
- **First of its kind in NYC**: "I'm always surprised that we are the first one in New York City" (P12); "the biggest city in the world... not the most advanced" (P12)
- **Scalable to other locations**: "I hope I can see it in more towns and more areas... Central Park or Times Square" (P04)

### 10.3 Technology as Cultural Bridge
- **"AR connects people instead of isolating them"** (P20) — bold statement about outdoor AR
- **"It's the right way to let this technology connect people instead of isolating people in their bubbles"** (P20)
- **Cross-cultural appeal**: non-Chinese participants also engaged and curious
- **Generational bridge**: "I met a little girl... she started trying out AR at the age of four. I feel like there's a generational gap" (P22)
